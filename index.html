Keystones to my recovery

Acceptance

The first thing I had to do was to accept that I was powerless over my disease. Once I was able to do that, I was able to find acceptance in all things throughout my life. This helped with my debilitating anxiety and eased my daily stresses.
"Until I could accept my alcoholism, I could not stay sober; unless I accept my life completely on life's terms, I cannot be happy."-
Big Book - Alcoholics Anonymous

Support

There are lots of places to get support throughout your Journey. Below are many that I have used on my journey. AA meetings, Sponsorship, being of service to others, Inpatient treatment, Day treatment, Intensive outpatient treatment, Individual therapy, Group therapy, friends, family & my amazing girlfriend Sydney

Goals

Setting & achieving goals was a big part of my success throughout the asap program and my personal recovery. Big or small, having goals gave my mind, body and spirt something to focus on instead of the negative self talk or cravings.
"It is good to have an end to journey toward; but it is the journey that matters, in the end."-
Ernest Hemingway

What else helped?

Exercise

Exercise was very helpful especially early on in recovery. Some of the benefits that I saw were... Stress reduction, better sleep, increased energy, improved mood and mental clarity, improved production of natural endorphins, reduced cravings & overall better health.

Honesty

I had to get honest with myself about the reasons why I used drugs. Once I was able to pinpoint those reasons, then I was able to be honest with others about what was going on inside of my head. Then I was able to do "the real work" and get the proper therapy and support that I needed to have a successful recovery.

Mindfulness & Meditation

Mindfulness has become one of my key coping strategies. I am able to focus on staying in the present moment by becoming aware of my breath and whats going on inside of my body. Then watching my thoughts come and go without judging them or giving them any power over me in that moment. By using mindfulness I am able to stay in the present moment, notice my anxieties, cravings and stressors but not let them control me or my actions.
