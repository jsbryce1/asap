Keystones to my recovery

Acceptance

The first thing I had to do was to accept that I was powerless over my disease. Once I was able to able to accept my powerlessness, then I was able to find acceptance in all things. This helped with my debilitating anxiety and eased my daily stresses.

"Until I could accept my alcoholism, I could not stay sober; unless I accept my life completely on life's terms, I cannot be happy."—
Big Book - Alcoholics Anonymous

Support

There are many places to get support throughout your Journey. Below are some that I have used on my own. AA meetings, Sponsorship, being of service to others, Inpatient treatment, Day treatment, Intensive outpatient treatment, Individual therapy, Group therapy, friends, family & my amazingly supportive girlfriend Sydney.

"The ultimate measure of a man is not where he stands in moments of comfort and convenience, but where her stands at times of challenge and controversy."—
Martin Luther King Jr.

Goals

Setting & achieving goals was a big part of my success throughout the asap program and my personal recovery. Big or small, having goals gave my mind, body and spirt something to focus on, instead of the negative self talk or cravings.

"It is good to have an end to journey toward; but it is the journey that matters, in the end."—
Ernest Hemingway

What else helped?

Exercise

Exercise was very helpful especially early on in recovery. Some of the benefits that I saw were Stress reduction, better sleep, increased energy, improved mood and mental clarity, better mindset, reduced cravings & overall better health.

Honesty

I had to get honest with myself about the reasons why I used drugs. Once I was able to pinpoint those reasons, I was then able to be honest with others about what was going on inside of my head. Once I got honest, I was able to do "the real work" and get the proper therapy and support that I needed to have a successful recovery.

Mindfulness & Meditation

Mindfulness has become one of the most effective coping strategies that I utilize. When I start to feel stressed or anxious, I am able to stay in the present moment by... focusing on my breath, becoming aware of my thoughts, focusing on my surroundings and how my body is feeling. During this time I am able to watch my thoughts come and go without judging them or giving them any power over me in that moment. By using mindfulness I am able to stay in the present moment, notice any anxieties, cravings and stressors that are currently happening, but not let them control me or my actions.

& Much more

There are many things that contributed to my recovery and success that are not listed here. The first step of Alcoholics Anonymous states, "We admitted we were powerless over alcohol and that our lives had become unmanageable." This was the first step that I had to take before I could start my recovery. Everyone has a different story and a different journey, these are just some of the many tools that I found helpful and hopefully they can help you as well. Recovery is a Journey not a race. Don't compare, take suggestions & take it one day at a time.
